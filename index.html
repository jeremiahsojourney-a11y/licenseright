Demystifying the 360 Deal: A Guide for Independent Artists

Youâ€™ve heard the term whispered in industry circles, often with a mix of awe and caution: the "360 deal." As an independent artist, it represents one of the most significant and potentially life-altering contracts you could ever sign. But what is it, really? And more importantly, is it right for you?

A 360 deal, also known as a "multiple rights deal," is a contract between a record label and an artist where the label receives a percentage of revenue from all of the artist's activities, not just recorded music sales. This includes touring, merchandise, publishing, endorsements, film and TV appearances, and more. In essence, the label invests in the artist as a complete brand and, in return, gets a piece of every pie.

The Pros: Why Artists Sign Them

  • Significant Investment: Labels are more willing to invest heavily in an artist's development, marketing, and touring because they have more ways to recoup their money.
  • Unified Team: Your career is managed by a single, powerful entity. Your tour promotion, album release, and merchandise are all handled by one team, creating a cohesive brand strategy.
  • Leverage: A major label's backing can open doors to opportunities that are nearly impossible to access as an independent artist, from major festival slots to brand partnerships.

The Cons: The Risks Involved

  • Lower Percentage: You will be giving away a piece of everything. The percentage might be small (typically 10-25%), but it applies to revenue streams you might have previously kept 100% of.
  • Loss of Control: The label has a say in all aspects of your career. This can lead to creative differences and pressure to accept opportunities you might otherwise decline.
  • Long-Term Commitment: These are not short-term contracts. You could be tied to the label for many years, across multiple albums and projects.

The bottom line: A 360 deal is a high-risk, high-reward proposition. It can provide the fuel to launch a global career, but it comes at the cost of revenue and autonomy. Before ever considering one, it's crucial to have expert legal advice and a clear understanding of your long-term goals.

Beyond the Majors: The Best Independent Record Labels for New Artists in 2025

For decades, the dream of every new artist was to get signed by a major label. But the digital age has shattered that paradigm. Today, some of the most innovative, artist-friendly, and culturally relevant music is released by independent labels. They offer creative freedom, fair deals, and a passionate team that genuinely believes in the music.

But with thousands of indie labels out there, where do you even start? We've compiled a list of some of the most respected and forward-thinking labels that have a proven track record of discovering and nurturing new talent.

Labels to Watch:

  • Secretly Canadian: Known for their impeccable taste and diverse roster (including artists like ANOHNI and Whitney), they have a reputation for building long-term careers.
  • Glassnote Records: Home to global superstars like Mumford & Sons and CHVRCHES, Glassnote proves that an indie can have major-label impact with a boutique, artist-first approach.
  • Partisan Records: A powerhouse of post-punk and indie rock, Partisan is celebrated for its bold signings and commitment to authentic, boundary-pushing artists like IDLES and Fontaines D.C.
  • Ninja Tune: For artists in the electronic, hip-hop, and experimental space, Ninja Tune is a legendary label that has remained at the cutting edge for over 30 years.
  • Ghostly International: A unique label that operates at the intersection of electronic music and visual art, Ghostly is perfect for artists who see their music as part of a larger aesthetic world.

How to get their attention? Forget mass emails. Research the specific A&R at each label. Follow them on social media. Understand their roster and their ethos. A targeted, personal approach that shows you've done your homework is infinitely more effective than a demo blast.

Finding a Champion: Top Music Publishing Companies for Independent Artists

As an independent artist, you know that a great song is only the beginning. Getting that song heard, placed in films or TV shows, and earning royalties is where a music publisher becomes your most valuable partner. A good publisher doesn't just collect money; they actively champion your music and create new opportunities.

While the major publishers (Sony, Universal, Warner Chappell) are giants, many independent publishers are more accessible and offer a more hands-on approach for developing writers. Here are some of the best publishing companies for indie artists who are serious about the business of their music.

Publishers Who Partner with Indies:

  • Kobalt Music Group: A technology-driven publisher famous for its transparency and creator-first deals. They offer administration deals that allow you to retain ownership of your copyrightsâ€”a game-changer for many artists.
  • Downtown Music Publishing: With a fantastic creative team, Downtown is known for actively pitching songs for sync placements and helping artists secure high-profile collaborations.
  • Sentric Music: An excellent starting point for emerging songwriters. Sentric offers a flexible, 28-day rolling contract that allows you to collect royalties without getting locked into a long-term deal. It's a great way to professionalize your publishing.
  • Wixen Music Publishing: A staunch defender of songwriter rights, Wixen has a reputation for meticulous administration and ensuring every penny is collected for their clients.

Before approaching a publisher, make sure your business is in order. Register your works with a PRO (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC), have clear documentation of your writer splits, and create a small, curated playlist of your absolute best work. Professionalism and preparation will make you stand out.
